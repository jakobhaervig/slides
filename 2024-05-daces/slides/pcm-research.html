An OpenTerrace example of a storage system with phase-change material

DaCES Thermal Energy Storage working group

14 May 2024

Case study

  • Small scale district heating system combining:
    • Heat pump
    • Water storage tank with PCM capsules
  • Idea: Use heat pump to produce heat when electricity price is low and store in water/PCM tank
  • Challenge: The phase-change complicates the transient response of the PCM storage. Makes it difficult to control the system effectively
  • Goal: Use OpenTerrace to predict the transient response

Validation case

  • Physical setup
    • Cylindrical storage tank with water ($H=1.9$ m, $D=0.79$ m)
    • Uniform initial temperature ($T_\text{init}=47^\circ$C)
    • Constant and uniform inlet temperature ($T_\text{in}=67^\circ$C)
    • Packing density of PCM modules in storage tank ($\phi=0.35$)
    • PCM modules assumed to be hollow spheres ($R_\text{inner}=5.53$ cm and $R_\text{outer}=6.65$)
    • Constant mass flow rate ($\dot{m}=360$ kg/h)
  • Numerical details
    • Tank discretised in 290 volume elements, PCM material discretised in 40 volume elements
    • Heat transfer coefficient assumed constant ($h=70$ W/m$^2$K)
    • Time step of 0.1 s
    • Simulating 800 min
    • Solved in approx 2 min
